Born in Frankfurt on 12 September 1957, The music of Hans Zimmer moves millions of people around the world. The number of Zimmer listeners who enjoy his encaptivating music even without films is constantly growing, with the word’s largest concert halls and football stadiums filled with his fans. Born in Germany, Hans Zimmer is one of Hollywood's most unique musical talents who in his early days featured in the Buggles music video "Video Killed the Radio Star” which went on to be a worldwide success on MTV. During these years, Zimmer was a skilled expert in combining both old and new musical technologies. Today, this work has earned him a name for integrating the electronic music world into traditional orchestral compositions. Zimmer composed the score for Best Picture Oscar recipient, Driving Miss Daisy, starring Jessica Tandy and Morgan Freeman in the 80s among with other Oscar nominated films as well. In 2003, Zimmer did his 100th film inclusion for the film The Last Samurai, starring Tom Cruise, for which he got both a Golden Globe and Broadcast Film Critics nomination. His more recent major recognitions include Gladiator, Batman: The Dark Knight, Pirates of the Carribean, Interstellar, and many more.
